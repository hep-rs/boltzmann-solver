Contribution from a single particle in the Universe.

The particle can be either a fermion or boson with arbitrary (non-integral) degrees of freedom. The degrees of freedom are (typically):

  • 2 for a fermion;
  • 1 for a real scalar;
  • 2 for a complex scalar;
  • 2 for a massless boson;
  • 3 for a massive boson (dependent on the gauge).

The contribution of the particle to the entropy of the Universe is based on the data from arXiv:1609.04979.

Note that this always assumes the particle to be free and does not work if the particle forms bound states below a particular energy scale (such as with coloured particles in the Standard Model).
